Evolution of F1 cars

F1 cars have come a long way, overtime they have become wider more aerodynamic, more power full. In the start the start of formula one it took cars nearly 7 second to reach 60 miles an hour however with more horsepower and aerodynamic they can hit 60 mile in less than 2 second and experience up to 5 g in the corner. F1 cars in past could only hit 140-150 mile an hour, but in today's races cars can hit over 200 miles per hour on a regular basis on the straights.

A Formula One car is a single-seat, open cockpit, open-wheel racing car with substantial front and rear wings, and an engine positioned behind the driver, intended to be used in competition at Formula One racing events. The regulations governing the cars are unique to the championship. The Formula One regulations specify that cars must be constructed by the racing teams themselves, though the design and manufacture can be outsourced.
